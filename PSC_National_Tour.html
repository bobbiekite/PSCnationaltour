The National Connection Tour

A briefing for leadership: what it is, why it matters, and how it works. A strategic, multi-audience field effort that extends a University of Denver college into a national community.
Prepared for the Provost and colleagues · Working draft
What

A strategic series of city visits for our programs and OLLI.

Across a year-long effort, PSC carries its full portfolio into strategically selected cities where our people already are.

In each market the tour runs three coordinated activities: meeting alumni, students, and adjunct faculty in person; placing program collateral with community colleges and employers; and reaching older learners for OLLI, including the OLLI members and alumni-OLLI members we already have across the country. It uses the PSC Professional Passport as its connective thread, with each visit anchored to a nearby National Park Service site, the same sites our learners can visit with the America the Beautiful pass included in the Passport.

Why

Reach, retention, and pipeline, together.

PSC is a University of Denver college serving a national, working-adult audience online. This tour carries DU's 4D Experience to that community, and the Passport turns geography into an asset rather than a limit.

Operational and relational excellenceOperational, because one coordinated effort unites the data and work of advancement, student success, marketing and communications, recruitment, advising, academic affairs, and faculty affairs into a single instrument, rather than seven separate efforts. Relational, because every visit deepens real human connection at the same time. Aligned with the college's directionThe work supports our innovation and differentiation priorities, the reduced-credit ABPS pathway, and our enrollment and retention goals, using the employer tuition-benefit channel (IRS §127, $5,250 per year) as a force multiplier.

How

A disciplined, repeatable method.

This is not improvised travel. It runs on a scoring model, a fixed city playbook, and a measurement frame, so every market is chosen deliberately and every visit is comparable.

Choose markets with a scoring model. Five data layers rank each city. Three are magnets that attract us (alumni density, tuition-reimbursement employers, and proximity to a National Park Service anchor) and two are filters that gate a motion (a local OLLI tempers the OLLI push, and a bachelor-granting community college is screened out as a competitor).
Run one repeatable playbook per city. A three-phase template: pre-visit outreach and booking, a one to two day visit running the three motions, and post-visit follow-up with every contact routed to the right next step.
Schedule with real lead time. Because visits are planned well ahead, each city has a known window, which tells us when to send invitations and confirm meetings in advance.
Measure each motion on its own terms. Relationship, pipeline, and OLLI each have their own scoreboard, so results are clear and the next visit starts smarter.

01 · The Through-Line

Anchored to the 4D Experience, carried by the educational journey.

Everything PSC builds is anchored to DU's 4D Experience: Deepen Intellect, Discover Character, Develop Well-Being, and Design Careers and lives of purpose. This tour is no different. It carries those four dimensions out into the country.

The vehicle is the educational journey. Every visit anchors to a nearby National Park Service site, where a structured encounter, not tourism, delivers all four dimensions at once: Deepen through the connection to coursework, Discover through critical reflection at heritage and civic sites, Develop through nature and well-being, and Design through coaching and portfolio work. The site is not the lesson; the encounter is.

That is why the educational journey is the anchor and not a backdrop. It is the glue that holds a national, mostly online community together no matter where people live, and an instrument for continued lifelong learning for every population, current students and older OLLI learners alike. The America the Beautiful pass in the Passport puts a journey within reach of almost any city we choose.

Onto that anchor we bundle three distinct motions that share geography but serve different audiences and products. They travel together. They convert separately. Run this way, the tour is an exercise in operational and relational excellence: it unites the data and effort of many DU teams, advancement, student success, marketing and communications, recruitment, advising, academic affairs, and faculty affairs, into one instrument while deepening human connection at every stop.

The four dimensions are the substance. The educational journey is how they travel. The Passport is the reason it all connects back to Denver.

What we are actually doing in each city.

Serves: OLLI retention · OLLI @ DU online · Denver visitation

The anchor that carries all three

We pick the cities with the scoring tool, then anchor each one to a nearby National Park Service site, the Passport demo. Because almost every market has an NPS site within reach, the anchor travels with us wherever the tool points, and the visit itself becomes the demonstration.

The tool also decides which of the three motions to run in each market. We will not run all three everywhere, and which cities earn a visit is an output of the scoring, not a fixed itinerary.

03 · The Market-Match Tool

Magnets pull us in. Filters keep us out.

The five data layers do not all do the same job. Three are magnets that attract us to a market. Two are filters that gate a specific motion, which is the insight that makes the tool smart instead of a spreadsheet.

+ Opportunity Magnets
Higher means more reason to go
People densityGeocoded alumni, current students, adjunct faculty, and OLLI members (flagging alumni-OLLI members) within roughly 60 miles. The core of Motions A and C and our referral engine.
NPS anchor proximityDistance to the nearest one or two NPS sites. Closer means a cleaner Passport demo and a stronger stop.
Tuition-reimbursement employersCount and size of employers offering §127 benefits, from prior research. Fuels Motion B's B2B channel for ABPS and BACP.
– Competition Filters
Presence gates a motion, it does not kill the visit
Nearby OLLI (1 of ~124)A local OLLI in market means Motion C competes head to head. Gate OLLI scoring down and lean to online plus "come to Denver." White space (no nearby OLLI) is an OLLI green light.
Bachelor-granting community collegesCCs that grant their own bachelor's are competitors, not partners. Keep and score the non-bachelor CCs as transfer targets, deprioritize the rest for drops.

The scoring model

Each market gets a composite score built from weighted magnets, then per-motion gates are applied so the tool tells us not just whether to go, but which plays to run when we get there. Suggested starting weights, tune to our goals:

Market Score = 0.35·People + 0.25·Employers + 0.20·NPS + 0.20·NonBachCC
OLLI sub-score = (Market Score) × OLLI gate  [gate = 1.0 if no OLLI within ~50 mi, 0.4 if one exists, favor online plus Denver-visit messaging either way]
All magnet inputs are normalized 0 to 1 across the national set before weighting, so one huge metro does not swamp the model.

From scores to a route: the corridor builder

Ranking markets gives a shopping list, not a route. The second step keeps the high-scoring markets that sit along a drivable corridor and orders them to minimize backtracking, the same optimization used to build the mapped route. Each corridor becomes a self-contained mini-campaign we can run, measure, and repeat.

Worked example, how the tool reads a market

Take a hypothetical mid-size market. Good alumni, student, and OLLI-member density (strong A and C). Several employers with §127 tuition benefits (strong B-employer). Two National Park Service sites within reach (strong anchor, an easy Passport demo). A couple of non-bachelor community colleges nearby for transfer (good B-pipeline).

The gate that matters: if our OLLI list flags a local Osher in that market, the OLLI prospecting score gates down, so we lean on connecting with existing members plus online OLLI and a Denver-visit invitation rather than a hard new-member push. The relationship and pipeline motions still fire fully, so the market earns a visit.

04 · The Repeatable City Playbook

One template. Run it in every market.

Once the tool picks a city, we do not reinvent the visit. We run the same three-phase play, so the effort scales and every market is comparable.

Phase 0 · 4 to 6 weeks out

Pre-visit: pull, segment, book, invite

Pull the market's data slice from the tool. Segment the contact list into the three motions. Book one warm venue (coffee shop, restaurant, or an alum's workplace). Calendar the Motion B meetings (CC drops and employer HR or L&D) and Motion C drops (independent living and chamber). Send personal invites: alumni and students get the "come be connected" homecoming note, adjunct faculty get the CoP launch and Adjunct Professorial Series note, employers and CCs get a partnership note.

Phase 1 · 1 to 2 days on the ground

The visit: gather, demo, drop, meet

A typical two-day stop. Evening 1: the alumni and student gathering over food (Motion A), with testimonial capture and referral asks, plus a focused adjunct faculty session to launch the CoP and answer appointment questions. Morning 2: an optional NPS Passport-journey mini-experience at the anchor site, the live demo, great for photos and content. Midday 2: CC collateral drops and a transfer conversation, then employer HR or L&D meetings (Motion B). Afternoon 2: OLLI drops at independent living and the chamber (Motion C). Adjust order to the market and who is available.

Phase 2 · within 1 week back

Post-visit: follow up, tag, route

Send thank-yous with one clear next step per contact. Tag everyone in the CRM by motion and outcome. Route adjunct faculty leads to CoP onboarding, transfer leads to admissions, employer leads to the B2B or Guild track, and OLLI leads to enrollment. Log the results against the KPIs so the next market starts smarter.

06 · Next Step

Hand over the data layers, build the live tool.

This is the strategy and the playbook. The scoring model above is the spec for the interactive market-match tool. With these five layers, the model becomes a working tool that ranks markets and proposes the cities worth a visit:

  1. People file, alumni, current students, adjunct faculty, and OLLI members (flagging alumni-OLLI members) with city or ZIP (geocodable).
  2. OLLI competition map, the roughly 124 national OLLIs with locations, the competition filter for Motion C, separate from our own member list above.
  3. NPS sites, the national site list with coordinates (the Passport anchors).
  4. Employer criteria plus research, prior §127 and tuition-reimbursement findings and the rule for flagging new ones.
  5. Community colleges, area CCs with a flag for whether they grant bachelor's degrees, so we keep the non-competing ones.

Even a partial set is enough to stand up a first version and a pilot market list.
